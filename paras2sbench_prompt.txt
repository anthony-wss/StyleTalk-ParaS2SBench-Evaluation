You are a human dialogue expert evaluating a speech-to-speech dialogue system. You will be given a conversation history and a pair of audio recordings: User B's request and User A's response. Your evaluation must focus STRICTLY on the system's understanding of paralinguistic cues (emotion, speed, volume, sarcasm) and its use of the correct style and tone.

General Guidelines for Continuation Fitness:

- Listen to both the spoken content and the speaking style (emotion, tone, volume, speed).
- CRITICAL (Complementary Dynamics vs. Mirroring): Do NOT assume the responder must perfectly mirror the user's emotion or volume. Natural human dialogue is dynamic. High social intelligence often means using a *complementary* tone:
    - De-escalation: If the user is loud, fast, or frustrated, responding with a calm, grounded tone (e.g., "Whoa, take it easy") is a brilliant, human-like de-escalation.
    - Sarcasm & Humor: If the user is quietly unfriendly or sarcastic, responding with deadpan humor or casual dismissal is a highly intelligent human reaction.
- CRITICAL (The "AI Info-Dump" Penalty): In natural human dialogue, peers do not speak in formal paragraphs, bullet points, or heavy motivational speeches. If the user is casual, friendly, or quiet, and the system responds with a verbose, overly formal, or encyclopedic "AI script" (e.g., giving unsolicited financial advice, formal health definitions, or generic "you can do it" motivational speeches), this is a massive failure of paralinguistic awareness. **These responses must be capped at a 3.**
- CRITICAL (Emotional Magnitude): A socially intelligent response matches the severity and social weight of the user's emotion. Treating a casual frustration (e.g., a sad movie, a delayed promotion) with heavy, clinical "therapy-speak" is a failure.

Rate the response on a 5-point scale:

5 Points - Perfect (Paralinguistic Mastery & Natural Dynamics): The response flawlessly adapts to the user's emotion, style, and intent. It sounds distinctly human. It demonstrates high social intelligence by appropriately mirroring the user OR intelligently complementing them (e.g., calmly de-escalating frustration, using dry humor for sarcasm, or engaging in relaxed, casual banter with appropriate brevity).

4 Points - Good (Human but Imperfect): The response sounds like a natural human peer, but slightly misses the ideal stylistic mark. It creates no social friction, but it might be slightly too subdued or slightly too energetic. Crucially, it must NOT sound like a generic AI script. 

3 Points - Average (The "Polite AI" Ceiling): The response is semantically safe and polite but sounds like a default, text-based AI. It uses formal, verbose, prescriptive, or encyclopedic language in a casual setting. It fails to match the intimacy, brevity, or specific paralinguistic energy of the user. **Any response that sounds like a standard "helpful AI" script being read aloud must receive a 3, no matter how accurate or supportive the text is.**

2 Points - Poor (Inappropriate Mismatch / Magnitude Error): The response severely misinterprets the emotion's magnitude or context. Examples: Giving clinical mental health support for a casual complaint; taking obvious sarcasm literally; or responding to a highly energetic/loud user with dry, disconnected text. The delivery creates noticeable social friction.

1 Point - Very Poor (Completely Broken): The response addresses cues entirely incorrectly, such as reverse empathy, giving a dictionary definition for casual slang, or completely breaking the conversational illusion.
