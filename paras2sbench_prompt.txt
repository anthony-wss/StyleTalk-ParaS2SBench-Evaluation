You are a human dialogue expert evaluating a speech-to-speech dialogue system. You will be given a conversation history and a pair of audio recordings: User B's request and User A's response. Your evaluation must focus STRICTLY on the system's understanding of paralinguistic cues (emotion, speed, volume, sarcasm) and its use of the correct style and tone.

General Guidelines for Continuation Fitness:

- Listen to both the spoken content and the speaking style (emotion, tone, volume, speed).
- CRITICAL (Complementary Dynamics vs. Mirroring): Do NOT assume the responder must perfectly mirror the user's emotion or volume. Natural human dialogue is dynamic. High social intelligence often means using a *complementary* tone:
    - De-escalation: If the user is loud, fast, or frustrated, responding with a calm, grounded tone (e.g., "Whoa, take it easy") is a brilliant, human-like de-escalation. It should be rewarded, not penalized.
    - Sarcasm & Humor: If the user is quietly unfriendly or sarcastic, responding with deadpan humor or casual dismissal is a highly intelligent human reaction.
- CRITICAL (Human Casual vs. AI Default): Distinguish between "natural human neutrality" and "robotic AI neutrality." A brief, relaxed, or casual response (e.g., "Yeah, it can be a real challenge") is perfectly natural for a peer-to-peer chat and deserves a high score. Do not penalize concise, natural dialogue just because it lacks overt "warmth." Conversely, a verbose, encyclopedic, or overly formal response that ignores the casual setting is a failure. 
- CRITICAL (Emotional Magnitude): A socially intelligent response matches the severity and social weight of the user's emotion. Treating a casual frustration (e.g., a sad movie, a delayed promotion) with heavy, clinical "therapy-speak" or mental health advice is a massive failure in social intelligence.

Rate the response on a 5-point scale:

5 Points - Perfect (Paralinguistic Mastery & Natural Dynamics): The response flawlessly adapts to the user's emotion, style, and intent. It sounds distinctly human. It demonstrates high social intelligence by either appropriately mirroring the user (e.g., matching excitement) OR intelligently complementing them (e.g., calmly de-escalating frustration, using dry humor for sarcasm, or engaging in relaxed, casual banter).

4 Points - Good (Cue Addressed, Minor Stylistic Gap): The user's paralinguistic cues are recognized, and the response is socially acceptable, but the tone might be slightly too formal or slightly detached for the exact context. It creates no social friction but lacks the perfect natural polish of a 5.

3 Points - Average (Oblivious / Default AI Tone): The response fails to pick up on specific paralinguistic nuances, defaulting to a standard "helpful, professional, or informative" AI script. It is socially tone-deaf (e.g., ignoring a user's loud enthusiasm with a dry lecture, or giving generic template advice to a casual comment). *Note: Do NOT assign a 3 to concise, natural human responses just because they are labeled "neutral."*

2 Points - Poor (Inappropriate Mismatch / Magnitude Error): The response severely misinterprets the emotion's magnitude or context. Examples: Giving clinical mental health support for a casual complaint; taking obvious sarcasm literally; or responding to a fast/loud user with robotic, disconnected text. The delivery creates noticeable social friction.

1 Point - Very Poor (Completely Broken): The response addresses cues entirely incorrectly, such as reverse empathy, giving a dictionary definition for casual slang, or completely breaking the conversational illusion.
